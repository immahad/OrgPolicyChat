# Paid Time Off (PTO) & Leave Policy

**Version:** 2.1  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** Human Resources Department  
**Last Reviewed:** December 15, 2023  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the Paid Time Off (PTO) entitlements and leave procedures for all full-time and part-time employees of Acme Corporation. The goal is to support employee well-being, work-life balance, and operational continuity.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all regular full-time employees, regular part-time employees (working 20+ hours/week), and employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period. Temporary, contract, and intern staff are not eligible for PTO unless explicitly stated in their contracts.

---

## 3. PTO Accrual

### 3.1 Full-Time Employees

PTO is accrued based on years of service:

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Days | Monthly Accrual |
|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| 0–1 year        | 15 days        | 1.25 days       |
| 1–3 years       | 18 days        | 1.50 days       |
| 3–5 years       | 21 days        | 1.75 days       |
| 5–10 years      | 25 days        | 2.08 days       |
| 10+ years       | 30 days        | 2.50 days       |

### 3.2 Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees (20–39 hours/week) accrue PTO on a pro-rated basis proportional to their scheduled hours relative to a 40-hour work week.

### 3.3 Accrual Cap

PTO accrual is capped at 1.5 times the employee's annual entitlement. Once the cap is reached, no further PTO accrues until the balance is reduced below the cap. Employees are encouraged to take time off regularly to avoid forfeiture of accrual.

---

## 4. PTO Usage

### 4.1 Minimum Usage Increment

PTO may be used in increments of no less than half a day (4 hours). Employees may not take less than half a day of PTO per occurrence.

### 4.2 Requesting PTO

- **Planned Leave (5+ days):** Must be submitted at least 4 weeks in advance via the HR portal.
- **Planned Leave (1–4 days):** Must be submitted at least 5 business days in advance.
- **Unplanned/Emergency Leave:** Employee must notify their direct manager by phone or email before 9:00 AM on the day of absence, or as soon as reasonably possible.

### 4.3 Approval Process

PTO requests are approved by the direct manager, subject to operational requirements. Managers must respond to PTO requests within 3 business days. Requests are generally approved unless business needs require the employee's presence, such as critical project deadlines, team coverage minimums, or company-wide events.

### 4.4 Blackout Periods

The company may designate certain periods as PTO blackout dates, during which PTO requests may be denied. Employees will be notified of blackout periods at least 60 days in advance. Typically, these occur during end-of-quarter financial close, major product launches, or peak operational seasons.

## 5. Public Holidays

Acme Corporation observes the following 11 paid public holidays per calendar year:

1. New Year's Day – January 1
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day – Third Monday in January
3. Presidents' Day – Third Monday in February
4. Memorial Day – Last Monday in May
5. Independence Day – July 4
6. Labor Day – First Monday in September
7. Thanksgiving Day – Fourth Thursday in November
8. Day After Thanksgiving – Fourth Friday in November
9. Christmas Eve – December 24
10. Christmas Day – December 25
11. New Year's Eve – December 31

When a public holiday falls on a Saturday, the preceding Friday is observed. When it falls on a Sunday, the following Monday is observed.

---

## 6. Sick Leave

### 6.1 Sick Leave Entitlement

Employees receive 10 days of paid sick leave per calendar year, separate from PTO. Sick leave does not accrue and resets on January 1 each year. Unused sick leave is not paid out upon termination.

### 6.2 Permitted Use of Sick Leave

Sick leave may be used for:
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical, dental, or mental health appointments
- Care for an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent) who is ill
- Recovery from a medical procedure

### 6.3 Documentation

For absences of 3 or more consecutive sick days, employees must provide a medical certificate from a licensed healthcare provider upon return. The certificate must confirm the employee's inability to work during the period of absence.

---

## 7. Bereavement Leave

Employees are entitled to paid bereavement leave upon the death of an immediate family member:

- **Spouse or domestic partner:** 5 paid days
- **Child (including stepchild or adopted child):** 5 paid days
- **Parent (including stepparent):** 3 paid days
- **Sibling:** 3 paid days
- **Grandparent or grandchild:** 2 paid days
- **In-laws (parent-in-law):** 2 paid days

For extended family or close friends, employees may use PTO at their manager's discretion.

---

## 8. Parental Leave

### 8.1 Maternity Leave

Birthing parents are entitled to 16 weeks of fully paid maternity leave, which may begin up to 2 weeks before the expected due date.

### 8.2 Paternity / Non-Birthing Parent Leave

Non-birthing parents are entitled to 4 weeks of fully paid parental leave within the first 6 months of the child's birth or adoption.

### 8.3 Adoption Leave

Employees who adopt a child under the age of 10 are entitled to 8 weeks of fully paid adoption leave.

### 8.4 Notice Requirements

Employees must notify HR at least 8 weeks before the anticipated start of parental leave, or as soon as reasonably possible in the event of an early birth.

---

## 9. Unpaid Leave of Absence

Employees who have exhausted all paid leave and require additional time off may request an unpaid leave of absence. Requests must be submitted in writing to HR and approved by both the direct manager and the HR Director. Unpaid leave may be granted for up to 12 weeks per year for qualifying reasons.

---

## 10. PTO Payout Upon Termination

Upon voluntary or involuntary termination, employees will be paid out for all accrued and unused PTO, in accordance with applicable state laws. Sick leave balances are not paid out. Employees who resign without providing the required notice period (2 weeks minimum) may forfeit accrued PTO at the company's discretion.

---

## 11. Policy Violations

Misuse of leave policies, including falsification of sick leave claims or bereavement leave, is subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.
