# Remote Work & Hybrid Work Policy

**Document ID:** HR-POL-002  
**Version:** 3.0  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** Human Resources Department & IT Department  
**Last Reviewed:** December 10, 2023  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines and expectations for remote and hybrid work arrangements at Acme Corporation. It ensures that employees can work productively outside the office while maintaining security, collaboration, and performance standards.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees whose roles are eligible for remote work. Eligibility is determined by job function, performance standing, and managerial discretion. Not all roles are eligible for remote work.

---

## 3. Work Arrangement Types

Acme Corporation supports three work arrangement categories:

### 3.1 Fully On-Site

Employees are required to work from an Acme Corporation office location five days per week. This applies to roles requiring physical presence, access to on-site equipment, or face-to-face client interaction.

### 3.2 Hybrid

Employees split their working time between the office and a remote location. The standard hybrid schedule requires a minimum of **3 days per week in the office**, with up to 2 days working remotely. Specific in-office days are determined by the employee's team and manager.

### 3.3 Fully Remote

Employees work entirely from a remote location. Fully remote arrangements are only granted for specialized roles or exceptional circumstances and require approval from the employee's VP-level manager and the HR Director. Fully remote employees are expected to travel to a company office at least **4 times per year** for team meetings, at the company's expense.

---

## 4. Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for a hybrid or remote work arrangement, employees must:

- Have completed at least **6 months** of employment
- Be in **good standing** (no active performance improvement plans or disciplinary actions)
- Have a **satisfactory or above** performance rating in their most recent review
- Have a suitable and secure home/remote workspace (see Section 6)
- Obtain written approval from their direct manager

Eligibility is reviewed annually. Remote/hybrid privileges may be revoked if performance declines or policy violations occur.

---

## 5. Requesting a Remote Work Arrangement

### 5.1 Application Process

Employees wishing to establish or modify a remote/hybrid arrangement must:

1. Submit a Remote Work Request Form via the HR portal
2. Discuss the arrangement with their direct manager
3. Receive written approval from the manager and HR

### 5.2 Trial Period

New remote/hybrid arrangements are subject to a **60-day trial period**, during which either the manager or employee may revert to a fully on-site arrangement without formal process.

### 5.3 Review and Renewal

Remote work arrangements are reviewed every **12 months** and are not automatically renewed. Employees must re-apply annually.

---

## 6. Remote Workspace Requirements

Employees working remotely must maintain a workspace that meets the following standards:

### 6.1 Physical Environment

- A dedicated, private workspace with a door that can be closed during work hours
- Adequate lighting, ergonomic seating, and a stable work surface
- Freedom from excessive noise, distractions, and interruptions during core hours
- The workspace must not be a public place (e.g., coffee shop, library) during calls involving confidential information

### 6.2 Internet Connectivity

- A minimum internet speed of **25 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload** is required
- A wired (Ethernet) connection is strongly recommended for video calls
- Employees are responsible for the cost of their home internet connection
- The company provides a monthly **internet stipend of $50** to eligible remote employees

### 6.3 Security Requirements

- Employees must use the company-provided VPN at all times when accessing company systems remotely
- The home network must be password-protected using WPA2 or WPA3 encryption
- Public Wi-Fi networks must never be used to access company systems without VPN
- All company data must be stored on company-approved systems, never on personal devices or personal cloud storage

---

## 7. Equipment and Technology

### 7.1 Company-Provided Equipment

The company provides the following equipment to eligible remote employees:

- Laptop computer (Windows or Mac, based on role)
- External monitor (one 24-inch monitor)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset for calls
- Webcam (if not built into the laptop)

### 7.2 Personal Equipment

Employees may not use personal computers for company work unless explicitly authorized by the IT Department. Personal mobile phones may be used for company email and messaging apps if enrolled in the company's Mobile Device Management (MDM) program.

### 7.3 Equipment Maintenance

Company equipment must be kept in good working condition. Damage resulting from negligence must be reported to IT immediately. The company covers repair costs for normal wear and tear. Employees are liable for damage caused by gross negligence or misuse.

### 7.4 Return of Equipment

All company equipment must be returned within **5 business days** of the employee's last day. Failure to return equipment may result in deductions from the final paycheck, to the extent permitted by law.

---

## 8. Core Hours and Availability

### 8.1 Core Hours

All employees, regardless of work arrangement, must be available and responsive during **core hours: 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM in their local time zone**, unless on approved leave. During core hours, employees must respond to messages within 30 minutes.

### 8.2 Flexible Hours

Outside of core hours, employees may structure their workday flexibly, provided they complete their required hours (40 hours/week for full-time) and meet all deadlines and commitments.

### 8.3 Time Zone Expectations

Employees working in time zones more than **3 hours** from their team's primary time zone must discuss scheduling expectations with their manager and may be required to adjust their working hours to ensure adequate overlap with the team.

---

## 9. Communication and Collaboration

Remote employees are expected to:

- Attend all team meetings via video (camera on, unless explicitly waived)
- Respond to emails within **4 hours** during business hours
- Respond to instant messages (Slack/Teams) within **30 minutes** during core hours
- Keep their calendar up-to-date and reflect their actual availability
- Inform their manager at the start of each day if they are working remotely on an unscheduled day

---

## 10. Performance and Accountability

Remote employees are held to the same performance standards as on-site employees. Managers evaluate remote employees based on output and results, not hours logged. If a remote employee's performance declines, the manager may require a return to the office, with 2 weeks' notice.

---

## 11. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy, including working from unauthorized locations, failure to maintain security standards, or misrepresenting work hours, may result in revocation of remote work privileges and disciplinary action up to and including termination.
