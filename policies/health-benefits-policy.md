## 1. Purpose

This policy describes the health, wellness, and financial benefits available to eligible Acme Corporation employees. Our benefits program is designed to support employees' physical, mental, and financial well-being.

---

## 2. Eligibility

Regular full-time employees working 30 or more hours per week are eligible for the full benefits package. Regular part-time employees working 20–29 hours per week are eligible for a partial benefits package (medical only). Benefits begin on the **first day of the month following the employee's hire date**.

---

## 3. Medical Insurance

### 3.1 Plan Options

Acme Corporation offers three medical plan options through BlueCross BlueShield:

| Plan        | Type  | Employee Premium/Month | Deductible | Out-of-Pocket Max |
|-------------|-------|------------------------|------------|-------------------|
| Plan A      | PPO   | $85                    | $500       | $3,000            |
| Plan B      | HDHP  | $40                    | $1,500     | $5,000            |
| Plan C      | HMO   | $60                    | $250       | $2,500            |

The company pays **80% of the employee premium** for all plans. Employees pay the remaining 20% via pre-tax payroll deduction.

### 3.2 Dependent Coverage

Employees may add eligible dependents (spouse/domestic partner, children up to age 26) to their medical plan. Dependent premiums are:

- Spouse/domestic partner: additional **$200/month** (employee pays 50%, company pays 50%)
- Each child: additional **$120/month** (employee pays 50%, company pays 50%)
- Family coverage cap: maximum **$450/month** employee contribution regardless of family size

### 3.3 Open Enrollment

Benefits elections are made during annual **Open Enrollment** (November each year) for the following calendar year. New hires have 30 days from their start date to make initial elections outside of Open Enrollment. Changes mid-year are only permitted following a **qualifying life event** (marriage, divorce, birth/adoption, loss of other coverage).

---

## 4. Dental Insurance

Acme Corporation provides dental coverage through Delta Dental:

- **Preventive care** (cleanings, exams, x-rays): covered at **100%** (no deductible)
- **Basic restorative care** (fillings, extractions): covered at **80%** after $100 deductible
- **Major restorative care** (crowns, bridges): covered at **50%** after $100 deductible
- **Orthodontia:** covered at **50%**, up to a lifetime maximum of $1,500

Employee premium: **$12/month** (company covers remaining cost). Dependent dental coverage is available at additional employee cost.

---

## 5. Vision Insurance

Vision coverage is provided through VSP:

- **Annual eye exam:** $10 copay
- **Frames/lenses:** Up to **$200 allowance** per year
- **Contact lenses:** Up to **$180 allowance** per year (in lieu of glasses)

Employee premium: **$6/month**. Dependent vision coverage available.

## 6. Retirement — 401(k) Plan

### 6.1 Plan Details

Acme Corporation offers a 401(k) retirement savings plan through Fidelity Investments. Employees may contribute on a pre-tax (Traditional) or post-tax (Roth) basis.

### 6.2 Company Match

Acme matches employee 401(k) contributions at:

- **100% match** on the first **2%** of salary contributed
- **50% match** on the next **2%** of salary contributed
- Maximum company match: **3% of base salary**

### 6.3 Eligibility and Vesting

Employees are eligible to enroll in the 401(k) from their **first day of employment**. Company matching contributions vest according to the following schedule:

| Years of Service | Vested Percentage |
|------------------|------------------|
| Less than 1 year | 0%               |
| 1 year           | 25%              |
| 2 years          | 50%              |
| 3 years          | 75%              |
| 4 years          | 100%             |

Employees are always 100% vested in their own contributions.

### 6.4 Contribution Limits

Annual contribution limits are set by the IRS. For 2024:
- Standard limit: **$23,000**
- Catch-up contribution (age 50+): additional **$7,500**

## 7. Life Insurance and Disability

### 7.1 Basic Life Insurance

Acme provides **company-paid basic life insurance** equal to **2x the employee's annual base salary**, up to a maximum benefit of $500,000. Coverage begins on the employee's first day. No medical exam is required.

### 7.2 Supplemental Life Insurance

Employees may purchase additional life insurance for themselves, their spouse, or their children during Open Enrollment. Amounts up to **4x salary** (for employee) are available without a medical exam.

### 7.3 Short-Term Disability (STD)

After a 7-day elimination period, STD pays **60% of base salary** for up to **13 weeks** for non-occupational illness or injury. Company-paid; no employee premium.

### 7.4 Long-Term Disability (LTD)

After STD benefits are exhausted (13 weeks), LTD pays **60% of base salary** (up to $10,000/month) for conditions lasting longer than 13 weeks, until age 65 or recovery. Company-paid.

## 8. Health Savings and Flexible Spending Accounts

### 8.1 Health Savings Account (HSA)

Employees enrolled in Plan B (HDHP) are eligible for an HSA. The company contributes **$500/year** to the employee's HSA. 2024 IRS contribution limits:
- Employee only: **$4,150**
- Family coverage: **$8,300**
- Catch-up (age 55+): additional **$1,000**

### 8.2 Flexible Spending Account (FSA)

Employees on Plan A or Plan C may enroll in a Healthcare FSA, contributing up to **$3,200/year** pre-tax. FSA funds must be used by March 15 of the following year (2.5-month grace period). Up to **$640** may be rolled over.

### 8.3 Dependent Care FSA

All eligible employees may contribute up to **$5,000/year** pre-tax to a Dependent Care FSA for qualifying dependent care expenses (childcare, elder care).

## 9. Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

Acme provides a confidential **Employee Assistance Program (EAP)** at no cost to employees and their household members. EAP services include:

- **Counseling:** Up to **6 free sessions per issue, per year** with a licensed therapist or counselor
- **Legal consultations:** Free 30-minute consultations on personal legal matters (not employment)
- **Financial counseling:** Free consultations on budgeting, debt management, and financial planning
- **Mental health crisis support:** 24/7 crisis line

EAP is completely confidential. Acme Corporation does not receive information about employees who use EAP services. Access via: **eap.acmecorp.com** or **1-800-555-EAP1** (24/7).

## 10. Wellness Program

### 10.1 Annual Wellness Stipend

All full-time employees receive an annual **wellness stipend of $600** (paid as $50/month) that can be used for:

- Gym memberships or fitness classes
- Mental health apps (Calm, Headspace, etc.)
- Fitness equipment (up to $300/year)
- Nutrition coaching or dietitian services
- Running races or athletic events registration

Claims are submitted via the Wellness portal on the HR intranet.

### 10.2 Wellness Challenges

HR organizes quarterly company-wide wellness challenges (step challenges, mindfulness weeks, etc.) with participation incentives including gift cards and additional wellness stipend contributions.

## 11. Additional Benefits

### 11.1 Commuter Benefits

Employees may contribute up to **$315/month** pre-tax for transit passes and qualified parking (2024 IRS limit).

### 11.2 Adoption Assistance

The company reimburses up to **$5,000** in adoption-related expenses per adoption event, including agency fees, legal fees, and court costs. Subject to manager and HR approval.

### 11.3 Pet Insurance

Employees may voluntarily enroll in group pet insurance through Nationwide Pet Insurance at discounted rates. This is fully employee-paid via payroll deduction. Available for dogs and cats.

### 11.4 Employee Discounts

Employees have access to a corporate discount platform offering discounts on retail, electronics, entertainment, and travel.
