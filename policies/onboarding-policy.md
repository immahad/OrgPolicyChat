# Employee Onboarding Policy

**Document ID:** HR-POL-005  
**Version:** 1.5  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** Human Resources Department  
**Last Reviewed:** November 25, 2023  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the onboarding process for new employees at Acme Corporation. A structured onboarding experience ensures that new hires are set up for success from day one by receiving the information, tools, and support they need to be productive and engaged.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all new full-time and part-time regular employees joining Acme Corporation. Contractors and temporary employees follow a modified onboarding process coordinated by the hiring manager.

---

## 3. Pre-Boarding (Before Start Date)

### 3.1 HR Responsibilities

Within 3 business days of offer acceptance, HR will:

- Send the new hire a welcome email with first-day instructions, office location, dress code, and parking/transit information
- Provide an onboarding checklist via the HR portal
- Collect required pre-employment documentation (I-9, W-4, direct deposit form, emergency contacts, signed offer letter)
- Initiate background check processing
- Set up the new hire's profile in the HR system (Workday)

### 3.2 IT Responsibilities

IT will provision the following at least **2 business days before** the start date:

- Company email account (firstname.lastname@acmecorp.com)
- Laptop configuration and software installation
- Access to core systems (Slack, Microsoft 365, HR portal, IT service desk)
- VPN credentials and setup instructions

New hires working on-site receive their equipment on Day 1. Remote new hires have equipment shipped to their address and should receive it 1–2 business days before their start date.

### 3.3 Manager Responsibilities

The hiring manager must, before the new hire's start date:

- Notify the team of the new hire's arrival and role
- Assign an onboarding buddy (a peer from the team)
- Prepare a 30-60-90 day plan
- Schedule first-week meetings including 1:1s with key team members and stakeholders
- Ensure workstation and access requests are submitted to IT

---

## 4. Day One

### 4.1 Welcome and Orientation

New hires participate in a **New Employee Orientation (NEO)** session on their first day, facilitated by HR. NEO covers:

- Company history, mission, values, and culture
- Overview of products and business units
- Key HR policies and benefits (this session counts as initial policy acknowledgment)
- Introduction to HR systems (Workday)
- Benefits enrollment overview
- Q&A session

NEO is conducted virtually for remote employees via Zoom and in-person for on-site employees.

### 4.2 IT Setup and Access

On Day 1, IT will:

- Provide hands-on setup assistance (on-site) or a virtual setup call (remote)
- Ensure the employee has access to all core tools
- Walk through security requirements (password, MFA, VPN)
- Provide the IT acceptable use policy

### 4.3 Manager Introduction

The hiring manager spends at least **1 hour** with the new hire on Day 1 to cover:

- Team structure, roles, and responsibilities
- Current team projects and priorities
- Communication norms and meeting schedules
- Role-specific expectations for the first 30 days

---

## 5. First Week

### 5.1 Mandatory Policy Training

Within the first 5 business days, new hires must complete the following mandatory training via the Learning Management System (LMS):

1. Information Security Awareness Training (2 hours)
2. Code of Conduct and Ethics (1 hour)
3. Anti-Harassment Training (1 hour)
4. Data Privacy Fundamentals (30 minutes)
5. Workplace Safety Training (30 minutes)

Completion of these trainings is tracked in Workday. Incomplete mandatory training by Day 5 will trigger an automated reminder and escalation to HR.

### 5.2 Benefits Enrollment

New employees have **30 days** from their hire date to enroll in benefits, including:

- Medical, dental, and vision insurance
- 401(k) retirement plan (company matches up to **4% of base salary**)
- Life insurance and disability coverage
- Flexible Spending Account (FSA) or Health Savings Account (HSA)
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

Benefits enrollment is completed through Workday. Employees who do not enroll within 30 days must wait until the next Open Enrollment period (annually in November) unless they experience a qualifying life event.

### 5.3 Onboarding Buddy

Each new hire is assigned an **onboarding buddy** — a peer from the team who has been at Acme for at least 6 months. The buddy is a resource for informal questions about culture, tools, and processes. The buddy and new hire are expected to meet at least **twice per week** during the first 30 days.

---

## 6. First 30 Days

### 6.1 30-Day Goals

Managers and new hires should align on **30-day goals** by the end of Day 1, documented in Workday. Typical 30-day goals include:

- Completing all mandatory trainings
- Learning the team's tools, workflows, and communication channels
- Meeting all key stakeholders and cross-functional partners
- Completing one small starter project or contributing to an existing project

### 6.2 30-Day Check-In

At the end of the first 30 days, the manager conducts a formal **30-day check-in meeting** to:

- Review progress against 30-day goals
- Gather the new hire's feedback on the onboarding experience
- Address any concerns or blockers
- Align on 60-day goals

---

## 7. Probationary Period (90 Days)

### 7.1 Definition

All new employees are subject to a **90-day probationary period** beginning on their hire date. During this period, employment is "at-will" with a shorter notice requirement, and certain benefits or programs may not yet be available (e.g., PTO access begins after Day 90, certain internal transfer eligibility).

### 7.2 90-Day Review

At the end of 90 days, the manager conducts a formal **90-day performance review** using the standard review template in Workday. The review assesses:

- Achievement of 30/60/90-day goals
- Demonstration of core values
- Quality of work and contributions
- Professionalism and culture fit

Based on the review, one of three outcomes is determined:

- **Probation passed:** Employee continues; transition to standard employment terms
- **Probation extended:** An additional 30 days with specific improvement areas defined
- **Probation not passed:** Employment ends; final paycheck and accrued PTO paid per company policy
