# Code of Conduct & Ethics Policy

**Document ID:** HR-POL-003  
**Version:** 2.0  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** Human Resources Department & Legal Department  
**Last Reviewed:** November 20, 2023  

---

## 1. Purpose

Acme Corporation is committed to maintaining a workplace built on integrity, respect, and ethical behavior. This Code of Conduct establishes the standards of behavior expected of all individuals who work at or represent Acme Corporation. It serves as a guide for decision-making and helps ensure that our actions reflect the values of the company.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees (full-time, part-time, temporary), contractors, consultants, interns, and board members of Acme Corporation, in all locations and in all contexts where they represent the company.

---

## 3. Core Values

Acme Corporation's conduct expectations are grounded in our five core values:

1. **Integrity** – We act honestly and transparently in all that we do.
2. **Respect** – We treat every person with dignity, fairness, and consideration.
3. **Accountability** – We take ownership of our actions and their outcomes.
4. **Collaboration** – We work together across teams, disciplines, and perspectives.
5. **Innovation** – We embrace new ideas and continuous improvement with intellectual curiosity.

---

## 4. Equal Opportunity and Anti-Discrimination

### 4.1 Equal Employment Opportunity

Acme Corporation is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate in hiring, compensation, advancement, training, or any other term of employment on the basis of:

- Race, color, ethnicity, or national origin
- Gender, gender identity, or gender expression
- Age (40 or older, as protected by applicable law)
- Sexual orientation
- Religion or belief
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Pregnancy or parental status
- Veteran or military status
- Any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, or local law

### 4.2 Reasonable Accommodations

Acme Corporation will provide reasonable accommodations for employees with disabilities or sincerely held religious beliefs, unless doing so would cause undue hardship. Employees seeking accommodations should contact HR.

---

## 5. Anti-Harassment Policy

### 5.1 Definition of Harassment

Harassment is unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment, or results in an adverse employment decision. Harassment includes but is not limited to:

- Verbal: Slurs, jokes, derogatory comments, or unwelcome teasing based on a protected characteristic
- Physical: Unwanted physical contact or threatening gestures
- Visual: Displaying offensive images, memes, or symbols
- Digital: Sending harassing emails, messages, or social media posts
- Sexual harassment: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature

### 5.2 Zero Tolerance

Acme Corporation has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment. All complaints will be taken seriously, investigated promptly, and resolved fairly. Retaliation against anyone who reports harassment in good faith is strictly prohibited.

### 5.3 Reporting Harassment

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it through one of the following channels:

- Directly to their manager (if the manager is not involved in the incident)
- To the HR Department at **hr@acmecorp.com**
- To the anonymous Ethics Hotline at **1-800-555-ETHICS** (available 24/7)
- Through the Ethics Reporting Portal on the intranet

All reports are treated confidentially to the fullest extent permitted by the investigation process.

## 6. Workplace Violence

Acme Corporation is committed to providing a safe workplace free from violence and threats. The following are prohibited on company premises or at company events:

- Physical violence or threats of violence against any person
- Intimidation, bullying, or aggressive behavior
- Possession of weapons of any kind on company property

Any threat or act of violence must be reported immediately to HR and/or security (x4000). In an emergency, call 911 first.

---

## 7. Conflicts of Interest

### 7.1 Definition

A conflict of interest arises when an employee's personal interests, financial interests, or relationships could influence—or appear to influence—their professional judgment or actions on behalf of the company.

### 7.2 Disclosure Requirement

Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to their manager and HR as soon as it arises. Examples include:

- Holding a financial interest in a company that is a supplier, customer, or competitor
- Having a close personal relationship with someone you manage, hire, or make compensation decisions about
- Outside employment or consulting that competes with Acme Corporation
- Serving on the board of a company that does business with Acme

### 7.3 Outside Employment

Employees may engage in outside employment or freelance work provided it:

- Does not compete with Acme Corporation's business
- Does not use Acme's confidential information
- Does not interfere with the employee's duties or performance
- Is disclosed to and approved by HR in advance

---

## 8. Gifts and Hospitality

### 8.1 Receiving Gifts

Employees may accept gifts from external parties (clients, vendors, partners) only if:

- The gift has a value of **$75 or less**
- It would not create a sense of obligation or influence a business decision
- It is transparent and would not embarrass the company if publicly disclosed

Cash, gift cards, and loans of money are never acceptable, regardless of value.

### 8.2 Giving Gifts

Gifts given to clients or partners must be of modest value (under $75), appropriate in nature, and pre-approved by a manager. Gifts must not be given in connection with a pending business decision.

### 8.3 Reporting

Gifts received that exceed $75 in value must be reported to HR within 5 business days. The company may require the gift to be returned, donated to charity, or retained by the company.

## 9. Confidentiality and Intellectual Property

### 9.1 Confidential Information

Employees have an obligation to protect Acme Corporation's confidential information. This includes business strategies, financial results, customer lists, product roadmaps, employee data, and any other non-public information. Confidential information must not be shared with unauthorized parties inside or outside the company.

This obligation continues even after employment ends.

### 9.2 Intellectual Property

All intellectual property (inventions, software, designs, written works, processes) created by employees in the course of their employment, or using company resources, is the property of Acme Corporation. Employees must sign an Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement as part of their onboarding.

---

## 10. Social Media and Public Communications

### 10.1 Personal Social Media

Employees may engage on personal social media as private individuals, provided they:

- Do not disclose confidential company information
- Do not make disparaging or defamatory statements about Acme, its customers, or colleagues
- Make clear that their views are personal and do not represent the company when discussing industry topics

### 10.2 Official Company Channels

Only employees authorized by the Marketing or Communications department may post on behalf of Acme Corporation on official social media accounts or issue press releases.

---

## 11. Substance Use

### 11.1 Drug and Alcohol Policy

Acme Corporation maintains a drug-free workplace. Employees must not:

- Report to work or remain on company premises under the influence of alcohol or controlled substances
- Use, possess, distribute, or sell illegal drugs on company property or during work hours
- Consume alcohol during working hours, except at approved company events where moderate alcohol consumption may be permitted

---

## 12. Reporting Violations and Non-Retaliation

Employees who in good faith report suspected violations of this Code of Conduct will not be subject to retaliation, regardless of the outcome of the investigation. Retaliation itself is a violation of this policy and will be treated accordingly.

To report violations:
- **Ethics Hotline:** 1-800-555-ETHICS (anonymous, 24/7)
- **Ethics Portal:** ethics.acmecorp.com
- **HR Department:** hr@acmecorp.com

---

## 13. Consequences of Violations

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

- Formal warning or written reprimand
- Suspension with or without pay
- Termination of employment
- Civil or criminal referral where laws have been violated

The severity of the consequence will be proportional to the nature and severity of the violation, and whether it was a first or repeat offense.

---

## 14. Annual Acknowledgment

All employees must read and electronically acknowledge this Code of Conduct annually. New employees must acknowledge it within their first 5 business days. Acknowledgment is recorded in the HR system.
