# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Document ID:** FIN-POL-001  
**Version:** 2.4  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** Finance Department  
**Last Reviewed:** December 5, 2023  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the reimbursement of business expenses incurred by employees of Acme Corporation in the course of conducting company business. The company is committed to reimbursing legitimate, reasonable, and necessary expenses promptly.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors (where specified in their contract), and officers of Acme Corporation who incur business expenses on behalf of the company.

---

## 3. General Principles

All business expenses must be:

- **Necessary:** Directly required for legitimate business purposes
- **Reasonable:** Appropriate in amount and nature for the business context
- **Authorized:** Pre-approved where required by this policy
- **Documented:** Supported by original receipts or equivalent documentation

Employees who submit fraudulent expense claims will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal proceedings.

---

## 4. Travel Expenses

### 4.1 Air Travel

- All domestic flights must be booked in **economy class**
- International flights over 6 hours may be booked in **business class** with VP-level pre-approval
- Flights must be booked at least **14 days in advance** to obtain the best available rates, where operationally possible
- Employees must book through the company's designated travel booking platform (Concur)
- Personal frequent flyer miles earned on company travel may be retained by the employee

### 4.2 Ground Transportation

- Taxi, rideshare (Uber/Lyft), or rental cars are reimbursable for business travel
- Personal vehicle use is reimbursed at the current **IRS standard mileage rate** (updated annually; $0.67/mile for 2024)
- Rental cars must be **compact or mid-size class** unless a larger vehicle is required for business purposes (e.g., transporting multiple colleagues or equipment)
- Employees must decline rental car collision insurance if the company credit card provides equivalent coverage (check with Finance)
- Airport parking is reimbursable; remote/economy parking is preferred over closer lots when cost-effective

### 4.3 Accommodation

- Hotel bookings must be made through Concur or an approved agency
- The maximum nightly hotel rate is:
  - **Tier 1 Cities** (New York, San Francisco, Boston, Washington D.C., Los Angeles): **$350/night**
  - **Tier 2 Cities** (Chicago, Seattle, Austin, Denver, Atlanta): **$250/night**
  - **All Other Locations:** **$175/night**
- Rates exceeding the above limits require pre-approval from a VP or above
- Employees may keep hotel loyalty points earned during company travel
- Laundry expenses are reimbursable for trips exceeding **5 consecutive nights** (maximum $20/day)

### 4.4 Per Diem and Meals During Travel

Employees on approved business travel are reimbursed for meals based on the following daily per diem rates:

| Meal         | Domestic Rate | International Rate |
|--------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Breakfast    | $20          | $25               |
| Lunch        | $25          | $35               |
| Dinner       | $50          | $70               |
| **Daily Total** | **$95**   | **$130**          |

- Receipts are required for all meal expenses over $25
- Alcohol is **not reimbursable** as a meal expense unless as part of an approved client entertainment event (see Section 6)
- If a meal is provided by the conference, event, or host, no per diem is payable for that meal

---

## 5. Meals and Entertainment (Non-Travel)

### 5.1 Business Meals

Meals with clients, prospects, or business partners are reimbursable with the following conditions:

- The business purpose and attendees must be documented on the expense report
- Maximum reimbursable amount: **$75 per person** (including tax and tip)
- Meals for internal team members only (without external guests) are limited to **$40 per person** and require manager approval
- Tips should not exceed **20%** of the pre-tax bill

### 5.2 Client Entertainment

Client entertainment (e.g., sporting events, concerts, dinners) must be pre-approved by a Director or above. The maximum reimbursable amount is **$200 per person per event**. Documentation must include the business purpose, client names, and how the event related to company business.

### 5.3 Team Celebrations and Meals

Departmental team lunches or celebrations must be pre-approved by the department head. The limit is **$30 per person** for lunches and **$60 per person** for dinners or special occasions.

---

## 6. Technology and Office Supplies

### 6.1 Office Supplies

Routine office supplies (pens, notebooks, paper) may be expensed up to **$50 per month** without pre-approval. Expenses over $50/month require manager approval.

### 6.2 Software and Subscriptions

Software subscriptions or licenses must be pre-approved by the IT Department before purchase. Unauthorized software purchases will not be reimbursed. Approved software must be used solely for business purposes.

### 6.3 Mobile Phone

- Employees in roles requiring constant business communication receive a **monthly mobile stipend of $75**
- Eligibility is determined by HR and the employee's manager
- Personal phone upgrades are not reimbursable

### 6.4 Home Office Equipment

Remote employees may expense home office setup costs up to **$500 per calendar year** (not per event) with manager pre-approval. This covers items such as a desk lamp, ergonomic accessories, or a monitor stand. Major equipment (monitors, chairs) is provided by the company directly (see Remote Work Policy HR-POL-002).

---

## 7. Conferences and Training

### 7.1 External Conferences

Attendance at external conferences must be pre-approved by the employee's manager. Allowable expenses include registration fees, travel, accommodation (per Section 4.3 limits), and meals (per diem rates apply). Employees are expected to share key learnings with their team upon return.

### 7.2 Professional Development

Employees may expense up to **$1,500 per calendar year** for job-related professional development, including online courses, certification exams, and books. Expenses over $1,500 require VP approval. See the Training and Development Policy (HR-POL-006) for further details.

---

## 8. Expense Reporting and Submission

### 8.1 Submission Deadlines

Expense reports must be submitted within **30 days** of incurring the expense. Expenses submitted more than 60 days after the expense date will not be reimbursed, except in exceptional circumstances approved by the Finance Director.

### 8.2 Required Documentation

All expense reports must include:
- Original itemized receipts (or photo of receipt via the Concur mobile app)
- Business purpose of the expense
- Names of attendees (for meals and entertainment)
- Manager approval for expenses exceeding individual limits

### 8.3 Expense Report Process

1. Submit expense report via **Concur** (preferred) or the Finance expense form
2. Direct manager reviews and approves
3. Finance reviews for policy compliance
4. Reimbursement is processed within **10 business days** of Finance approval via direct deposit to the employee's payroll account

### 8.4 Company Credit Card

Employees with a company-issued credit card must use it for all eligible business expenses. Personal credit cards should only be used when a company card is not accepted or was not available. Personal card expenses are reimbursed in the same manner as above.

---

## 9. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are **not** reimbursable:

- Personal meals not related to business travel or client entertainment
- Alcoholic beverages (except pre-approved client entertainment)
- Personal grooming (haircuts, toiletries, etc.)
- Personal entertainment (movies, personal subscriptions, etc.)
- Traffic fines, parking tickets, or traffic violations
- Lost or stolen personal belongings
- Spouse/partner travel or expenses unless pre-approved by VP+
- Gym memberships or fitness equipment (unless part of an approved wellness program)
- Upgrades to first-class travel not pre-approved
