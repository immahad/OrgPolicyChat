## 1. Purpose

This policy describes Acme Corporation's performance management process, including goal-setting, performance reviews, ratings, compensation decisions, and performance improvement procedures. It ensures a fair, consistent, and transparent approach to managing and developing employee performance.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all full-time and part-time regular employees. Temporary and contract employees are excluded unless otherwise specified in their contracts.

---

## 3. Performance Review Cycle

### 3.1 Annual Review

Acme Corporation conducts a formal annual performance review in **December** for the full calendar year (January–December). The review cycle includes:

1. **Self-Assessment** (due November 15): Employees complete a self-evaluation in the HR system (Workday), reflecting on achievements, challenges, and goals.
2. **Manager Assessment** (due November 30): Managers complete an evaluation of the employee, based on the employee's contributions against their goals.
3. **Calibration Sessions** (December 1–15): HR and department heads calibrate ratings across teams to ensure consistency and fairness.
4. **Review Conversations** (December 16–31): Managers discuss results with employees, including ratings, feedback, and goals for the following year.

### 3.2 Mid-Year Check-In

A non-rated mid-year check-in is conducted in **June**. This is a structured conversation between the employee and manager to assess progress on annual goals, identify obstacles, and adjust objectives if needed. A brief written summary must be submitted in Workday.

### 3.3 New Employee Reviews

Employees in their first year are reviewed at the **90-day** mark (end of probationary period) and again at **6 months**, in addition to any year-end review they qualify for. New employees who start after September 1 will not be rated in that year's annual cycle.

---

## 4. Performance Ratings

Acme Corporation uses a **five-point rating scale** for annual performance evaluations:

| Rating | Label                   | Description |
|--------|-------------------------|-------------|
| 5      | Exceptional             | Consistently far exceeds all expectations. Delivers landmark results, demonstrates outstanding leadership. Top 5–10% of peers. |
| 4      | Exceeds Expectations    | Regularly exceeds most or all expectations. High performer delivering strong impact. Top 15–25% of peers. |
| 3      | Meets Expectations      | Consistently meets all expectations. Solid, reliable contribution. Expected of all employees. |
| 2      | Partially Meets          | Meets some but not all expectations. Performance gaps that require attention and improvement. |
| 1      | Does Not Meet           | Consistently fails to meet expectations in multiple areas. Immediate improvement required. |

### 4.1 Rating Distribution Guidelines

While forced ranking is not required, HR provides calibration guidance to maintain approximate distributions:

- Rating 5: approximately 5–10% of the team
- Rating 4: approximately 20–25%
- Rating 3: approximately 55–65%
- Rating 2: approximately 10–15%
- Rating 1: approximately 1–5%

## 5. Goal-Setting

### 5.1 SMART Goals

All employees must set **SMART goals** (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) at the beginning of each calendar year, no later than **January 31**. Goals must be entered in Workday and approved by the employee's manager.

### 5.2 Number of Goals

Employees should set **3–5 meaningful goals** per year. Goals may include:

- Business objectives (quantitative performance targets)
- Project-based goals
- Development goals (skills, certifications, leadership)
- Company-wide or team-level goals (cascaded from leadership)

### 5.3 Goal Modification

Goals may be modified during the year due to significant changes in business priorities, role changes, or unforeseen circumstances. Any modifications must be agreed upon by the employee and manager and updated in Workday.

## 6. Compensation and Performance

### 6.1 Merit Increases

Annual salary increases (merit increases) are tied to performance ratings and the company's annual compensation budget. General guidelines for merit increases:

| Rating | Typical Merit Increase Range |
|--------|------------------------------|
| 5 – Exceptional           | 5.0% – 8.0% |
| 4 – Exceeds Expectations  | 3.5% – 5.5% |
| 3 – Meets Expectations    | 2.0% – 3.5% |
| 2 – Partially Meets       | 0.0% – 1.0% |
| 1 – Does Not Meet         | 0.0% (no increase) |

Merit increases are effective **March 1** of the following year. Increases are subject to manager recommendation, HR review, and final budget approval.

### 6.2 Bonus Program

Employees at the manager level and above are eligible for an annual performance bonus. Bonus targets are defined by grade level and are calculated as a percentage of base salary. Bonuses are paid in **March** following the performance year, subject to the individual's rating and company financial performance.

| Level              | Bonus Target (% of Base Salary) |
|--------------------|----------------------------------|
| Manager            | 10%                              |
| Senior Manager     | 15%                              |
| Director           | 20%                              |
| VP                 | 25%                              |
| SVP / C-Suite      | 30%+                             |

### 6.3 Promotions

Promotions to a higher grade level require:

- A rating of **4 or 5** in the current or prior review cycle
- Demonstrated readiness for the next level (assessed by manager and HR)
- A position opening or business justification
- Approval by the department VP and HR

Promotions are typically effective **April 1** and include a salary adjustment of **8–15%**, subject to the new grade's salary range.

## 7. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

### 7.1 Purpose of a PIP

A Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) is a structured, time-bound plan designed to help an employee whose performance has fallen below expectations. It is intended as a supportive tool to give the employee a clear path to improvement.

### 7.2 When a PIP is Initiated

A PIP is typically initiated when an employee:

- Receives a rating of **1 or 2** in an annual review
- Exhibits persistent performance issues identified during regular feedback
- Fails to meet specific, documented performance expectations after informal coaching

### 7.3 PIP Process

1. **Initiation:** HR and the manager agree that a PIP is appropriate
2. **Documentation:** The PIP document is created outlining specific performance gaps, improvement targets, and timelines
3. **Meeting:** The manager presents the PIP to the employee; the employee has the right to respond in writing
4. **Duration:** PIPs typically run for **60–90 days**, depending on the nature of the issues
5. **Check-ins:** Bi-weekly documented check-ins between manager and employee
6. **Outcome:** At the end of the PIP period, one of three outcomes is determined:
   - **Successfully completed:** Performance has improved; employee exits the PIP
   - **Extended:** Partial improvement; PIP extended by up to 30 additional days
   - **Not completed:** Performance did not sufficiently improve; employment may be terminated

### 7.4 Employee Rights During a PIP

During a PIP, employees have the right to:

- Receive clear, written documentation of performance expectations
- Meet regularly with their manager and HR
- Request a meeting with HR if they feel the PIP is unfair
- Continue to perform their normal duties unless otherwise specified

---

## 8. Termination for Performance

Termination for performance reasons requires documentation of ongoing performance concerns, a PIP process (in most cases), and approval from HR and legal. Employees terminated for performance reasons are entitled to their earned salary and accrued PTO, per applicable law.

---

## 9. Appeals Process

Employees who believe their performance rating was unfair or inaccurate may appeal through the following process:

1. Submit a written appeal to HR within **10 business days** of receiving the final rating
2. HR will review the documentation and conduct a separate review interview
3. HR may uphold, modify, or overturn the rating based on the evidence
4. The HR Director's decision on the appeal is final
