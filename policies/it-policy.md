# IT Acceptable Use & Device Policy

**Document ID:** IT-POL-002  
**Version:** 2.2  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** IT Department  
**Last Reviewed:** December 8, 2023  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of Acme Corporation's information technology resources, including hardware, software, networks, and communications systems. It aims to protect the company's assets, ensure operational efficiency, and maintain a secure and productive work environment.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, interns, and third-party users who are provided with or access Acme Corporation's IT resources, regardless of device ownership or physical location.

---

## 3. Company-Provided Hardware

### 3.1 Laptop and Desktop Computers

Every full-time employee is provided with a company-issued laptop upon hire. The standard equipment provided is:

- **Engineering / Design roles:** 16-inch MacBook Pro (16 GB RAM, 512 GB SSD)
- **All other roles:** 14-inch MacBook Air or Dell Latitude (8 GB RAM, 256 GB SSD)

Upgrades to the standard configuration must be requested from IT and require manager and Finance approval. Equipment refresh cycles are every **3 years**.

### 3.2 Peripherals

Standard peripheral equipment issued by IT includes:

- One external monitor (24-inch or 27-inch, depending on role)
- Wireless keyboard and mouse
- USB-C docking station (for desk setups)
- Headset (for remote/hybrid employees)

Additional peripherals may be requested through the IT Service Desk portal.

### 3.3 Mobile Phones

Company mobile phones are issued to employees whose roles require constant business communication (e.g., sales, executive, on-call engineering). Eligibility is approved by the employee's VP. Employees not issued a company phone may use personal devices for business communication subject to MDM enrollment (see Section 5).

---

## 4. Software and Licensing

### 4.1 Approved Software

All software installed on company devices must be approved by IT. The current list of approved software is published on the IT intranet page. Commonly approved software includes:

- Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams)
- Slack
- Zoom
- Google Chrome / Mozilla Firefox
- Adobe Acrobat Reader
- 1Password (password manager)
- Cisco AnyConnect (VPN client)
- Jira and Confluence (project management)
- GitHub / GitLab
- Figma (design roles)
- VS Code / IntelliJ IDEA (engineering roles)

### 4.2 Requesting New Software

To request software not currently on the approved list:

1. Submit a Software Request via the IT Service Desk portal
2. IT evaluates security, licensing, and compatibility
3. If approved, IT will procure and install the software centrally
4. Response time: Standard requests are processed within **5 business days**; urgent requests within 2

### 4.3 Prohibited Software

The following types of software may not be installed on company devices under any circumstances:

- Peer-to-peer (P2P) file-sharing applications (BitTorrent, LimeWire, etc.)
- Unauthorized VPN clients other than the company-approved Cisco AnyConnect
- Browser extensions not vetted by IT
- Cryptocurrency mining software
- Keyloggers, screen recorders, or monitoring software not issued by IT
- Cracked, pirated, or unlicensed software of any kind

## 5. Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

### 5.1 BYOD Policy

Employees may use personal smartphones and tablets for company email, calendar, and communication applications (e.g., Microsoft Outlook, Teams, Slack). Personal laptops may not be used for company work without explicit IT authorization.

### 5.2 MDM Enrollment Requirements

To access company email or data on a personal device, the device must:

- Be enrolled in the company's MDM system (Microsoft Intune)
- Meet minimum OS requirements:
  - iOS: version 16 or later
  - Android: version 12 or later
- Have a screen lock (PIN, fingerprint, or Face ID) enabled
- Have device encryption enabled

### 5.3 MDM Capabilities

By enrolling in MDM, employees consent to the following:

- Remote wipe of **company data only** (not personal data) if the device is lost, stolen, or if employment ends
- Enforcement of security policies on the device (screen lock timeout, encryption)
- IT visibility into which company apps are installed and whether security policies are compliant

IT does not have access to personal data, photos, messages, or apps on enrolled personal devices.

## 6. Email and Communication Systems

### 6.1 Business Use

Company email (Microsoft Outlook/365) is provided for business communication. Employees should use professional language and exercise good judgment in all electronic communications, as business email may be subject to legal discovery.

### 6.2 Email Retention

Business emails are retained for **7 years** in accordance with the company's data retention policy. Employees must not delete emails related to ongoing legal matters, audits, or compliance reviews.

### 6.3 Phishing and Suspicious Email

- Do not click links or open attachments from unknown or unexpected senders
- Report suspicious emails using the "Report Phishing" button in Outlook
- If in doubt, contact the IT Security team before interacting with the email
- The IT team conducts quarterly phishing simulations to train staff

### 6.4 Instant Messaging (Slack/Teams)

Slack and Microsoft Teams are the primary platforms for internal instant messaging. Employees must:

- Use company-designated channels for team communication
- Avoid sharing confidential data in public Slack channels
- Ensure Direct Messages do not contain confidential information unless the conversation is in an approved, secure workspace

---

## 7. Internet and Network Usage

### 7.1 Internet Access

Internet access is provided for business purposes. Moderate personal browsing is permitted during non-work hours (e.g., lunch breaks), provided it does not violate the security policy or involve inappropriate content.

### 7.2 Bandwidth

High-bandwidth personal activities (streaming personal video, online gaming, large personal downloads) during working hours are prohibited on the company network, as they degrade performance for all users.

### 7.3 Blocked Content

The company's network filter blocks access to categories of content that are inappropriate or pose security risks, including:

- Adult/explicit content
- Known malware distribution sites
- Gambling sites
- Hacking tools and exploit databases

If a legitimate business site is incorrectly blocked, employees can submit an unblock request to IT.

## 9. End-of-Employment Procedures

Upon resignation or termination:

- All company IT accounts are deactivated on the employee's last day
- Company-issued devices must be returned to IT within **5 business days**
- Company data on personal devices will be remotely wiped (company data only)
- Employees must not transfer, copy, or retain any company data after their last day

---

## 10. Violations

Violations of this policy may result in immediate suspension of IT access, formal disciplinary action, and in cases of intentional misuse or data theft, legal action. IT audit logs are maintained and may be reviewed when policy violations are suspected.
